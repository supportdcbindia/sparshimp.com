How should SS 304 sheets and plates be cleaned and maintained?

Use a mild soap and water solution for cleaning. Avoid using harsh chemicals or abrasive cleaners that can scratch the surface. Dry the surface thoroughly after cleaning to prevent water spots and rust. Avoid exposing the stainless steel to high temperatures, as this can cause warping and discoloration. Store SS 304 sheets and plates in a dry, well-ventilated area to prevent rust and corrosion, If the surface becomes scratched or damaged, it is important to repair it promptly to prevent further damage and to maintain its corrosion resistance.

What is the standard size and thickness of SS 304 sheets and plates?

The standard size of SS 304 sheets and plates is 4' x 8' (1219mm x 2438mm). The standard thickness of SS 304 sheets/plates ranges from 0.5mm to 100mm, with typical thicknesses ranging from 0.7mm to 5mm. Custom sizes and thicknesses are also available, depending on the customer's requirements.
The weight and ease of carrying stainless steel 304 sheets and plates will depend on the size, thickness, and the quantity being transported. It may be necessary to use equipment such as a forklift, crane, or pallet jack to move large sheets and plates safely.

What is the difference between SS 304 and SS 316 sheet plates

Stainless steel sheet plates 304 contains 18% chromium and 8% nickel, while SS 316 contains 16% chromium, 10% nickel, and 2% molybdenum. SS 304 has excellent corrosion resistance in many environments, but SS 316 has improved resistance to chloride-ion corrosion. SS 304 and SS 316 have high strength and durability, but SS 316 has slightly better mechanical properties e.g. better temperature corrosion resistance. SS 316 is more difficult to weld than SS 304 due to its higher molybdenum content.
SS 304 sheets/plates is widely used in a variety of applications, including kitchen equipment, surgical instruments, architectural paneling, chemical containers, and food processing equipment, while SS 316 is typically used in applications like chemical processing equipment.
