How to determine the quality of SS 316 sheet plate?

When looking for a high-quality SS 316 sheet plate, it is important to note that the steel grade should be marked at least with '316ss' or even better '316L'. The presence of 'L' in the grade indicates that low carbon has been used to provide improved corrosion resistance compared to other grades of SS 316. Furthermore, an internationally recognised testing house should conduct mechanical, chemical, and/or micrographic testing. The report for these tests must include details such as strength values, composition, and size classification to ensure a premium product has been obtained. In addition, owing to its costliness it is advisable to ask the supplier for certificates of origin, traceability information, and evidence of test results. Checking the above details will make certain that the most suitable stainless steel plate is acquired at an appropriate price point.

How to properly store and maintain SS 316 sheet plates?

Storing and maintaining SS 316 sheet plates can seem overwhelming if you don't know the basics. One of the most important factors in caring for these plates is proper storage prior to use. Before even handling the plates, it is critical to confirm that they are in their proper storage environment - typically this includes temperatures below 60 degrees Celsius and humidity levels at 40-60%. In addition, try to keep them away from direct sunlight and any corrosive material. When handling your SS plate, do so carefully and inspect often it for any signs of damage or deformation. Also, make sure that the surface stays dry before storing it away again; otherwise, corrosion could occur over time. By following these simple maintenance steps you can properly store and maintain your SS 316 sheet plates for long-term use.
