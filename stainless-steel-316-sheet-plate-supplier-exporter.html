What is 316 Grade Stainless Steel?

As you know, stainless steel is an alloy; you must know what an alloy is before understanding the grades. In science, an alloy is a mixture of more than one metal or metal with a non-metallic substance. This means stainless steel is a mixture of a few metal or non-metallic substances, isn’t it? Yes, it is! Stainless steel of 316 grade is a composition of 16% chromium, 12 to 14% nickel, 2 to 3% molybdenum, and a very minimum percentage of carbon.

Do you know what makes the 316-grade stainless steel so demanding and famous? This is because a minimum of 16% chromium makes it corrosion-resistant. A thin layer of metal oxide is created over the steel sheet due to chromium's presence, which protects the stainless steel sheet from corrosive materials. This property of 316-grade stainless steel makes it a perfect choice for most industry and domestic uses.

What is 316 Stainless Steel Plate Used For?

316-grade stainless steel is a very popular material that has excellent corrosion resistance as well as high-temperature resistance. Therefore, it is widely and beneficially used in many different applications or industries, such as –

You must have seen that 316 stainless steel is widely used in the pulp and paper industry because the properties of 316-grade stainless steel help avoid iron contamination. Apart from the above-listed industries, you can see the application of 316 stainless steel in pressure vessels, furnace parts, valves, and pumps or environments with increased exposure to alkalis and acids.

What are the General Properties and Mechanical Properties of 316 Stainless Steel?

The 316-grade stainless steel is widely popular and used due to the alloy's general and mechanical properties, which benefit its application. Below mentioned are the general properties as well as mechanical properties of 316 stainless steel –

General Properties

316 stainless steel has some exceptional general properties such as –

  • Corrosion Resistance: As written above, 316 stainless steel has excellent corrosion properties. Due to the presence of molybdenum, if you keep it in an environment containing chloride, acid, or saltwater, it will resist corrosion.
  • High-Temperature Resistance: It can be used in extremely high temperatures and will never scale or oxidize. This property makes 316 stainless steel a perfect fit for using it in elevated temperatures.
  • Attractive Appearance: 316 stainless steel has a very attractive appearance. You must have seen these sheets are polished, and that’s why they are preferred for architecture or decoration purposes.
  • Hygienic Material: 316 Stainless steel is easy to clean and maintain. That’s why it is considered a hygienic material used in food processing and the pharmaceutical industry.

Mechanical Properties

The mechanical properties of 316-grade stainless steel are as follows –

  • Tensile Strength – Ranges from 515 to 680 Mpa (75-100 ksi)
  • Yield Strength – Ranges around 205 Mpa (30 ksi)
  • Elongation – 40% or above
  • Hardness – Ranges from 70 to 90 HRB (Rockwell B Scale) or 150 to 190 HV (Vickers scale)
  • Fatigue Strength – Possess excellent Fatigue Strength making it best fit for cyclic loading and repeated stress
  • Modulus of Elasticity – Approx. 193 GPa (28 x 10^6 psi)
The elongation property of 316 stainless steel allows it to go through deformation of any kind without breaking. The above-written mechanical may vary slightly due to external factors, including heat treatment or manufacturing process.
